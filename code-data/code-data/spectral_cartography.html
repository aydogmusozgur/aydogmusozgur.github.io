From Local Payoffs to Global Instabilities: A Spectral Cartography of Spatiotemporal Chaos in Canonical 2×2 Evolutionary Games

Ozgur H. Aydogmus  ·  Under Review

Abstract

We investigate the emergence of spatiotemporal chaos in canonical 2×2 evolutionary games defined on spatial domains, characterizing the transition from stable equilibria to complex dynamics via spectral methods. By mapping the parameter space of payoff matrices, we construct a spectral cartography that identifies regions of stability, pattern formation, and chaos. Our analysis reveals how local payoff structure drives global dynamical instabilities through nonlocal interactions.
